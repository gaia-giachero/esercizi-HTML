Volleyball

What Is Volleyball

☞ Volleyball is a team sport played between two teams☺, each with six players on the court and another six (or sometimes fewer) on the bench. The aim of the game is to score points by making the ball touch the ground in the opponent’s court (attacking phase), and to prevent the opposing team from doing the same (defensive phase).

It is also called “volley”, an abbreviation of the sport’s name in English, volleyball.

Volleyball at the Olympic Games

☞ Volleyball has been part of the Summer Olympic Games program since 1964 and is one of the most widely practiced sports.

Main International Volleyball Competitions

☞ The most important international competitions for national teams are the Men’s and Women’s Volleyball World Championships, both held every four years; the Men’s and Women’s Volleyball Nations League, both held annually; in Europe, the Men’s and Women’s European Volleyball Championships, both held every two years.

Among international club competitions are the Men’s and Women’s FIVB Volleyball Club World Championships; in Europe, the Men’s and Women’s CEV Champions League, all four held annually.
